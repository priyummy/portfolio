Virtual Board Game

A digitally implemented board game focused on strategic decision-making, resource tension, and emergent player interaction.

  • Role: Game Designer
  • Focus: Systems design, balance, player interaction
  • Tools: Unity, Tabletop Simulator

Design Goals

The primary goal of this project was to translate the strengths of physical board game design into a digital format without losing clarity, tension, or player agency.

Emphasis was placed on readable systems, meaningful trade-offs, and mechanics that encouraged interaction rather than isolation.

Core Systems

The core mechanics revolved around shared resources, asymmetric player roles, and turn-based decision structures that created emergent strategic outcomes over repeated play sessions.

Systems were iterated through frequent playtesting to ensure balance while preserving distinct player identities.

Iteration and Balancing

Early versions of the game suffered from dominant strategies that reduced long-term engagement.

Through structured playtests, mechanics were adjusted to introduce counterplay opportunities and dynamic shifts in power throughout the game.

What I Learned

This project strengthened my understanding of systemic balance and the importance of iteration informed by player behavior rather than designer intention alone.

I also gained experience adapting traditionally physical design problems into digital systems while maintaining accessibility and depth.
